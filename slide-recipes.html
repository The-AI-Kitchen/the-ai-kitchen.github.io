The four recipes

Transferable techniques, tool-agnostic. They work in Cowork, Claude Design, or any AI slide tool you pick up next year.

Recipe 1
Anchor to a template
Upload a .pptx or a reference image. Don't describe the template in prose.
Recipe 2
Specify slide types
"Title, then two-column, then bullets, then CTA" beats "make slides about X."
Recipe 3
Provide design tokens
Hex codes, font names, voice adjectives. Pass the style guide as context, not hope.
Recipe 4
Iterate at the slide level
First pass is a draft. Refine one slide at a time with targeted edits.

Hands-on exercises

You'll need a Claude Pro account for both. Both exercises use separate usage buckets, so they won't exhaust your chat limits.

Exercise 1: Convert a deck with the SCU skill

Target time: 30 min · Uses: Claude Cowork
  1. Open Claude Cowork on your Pro account.
  2. Go to Customize → Skills → "+" → "Upload a skill".
  3. Upload scu-brand-skill.zip from the starter kit above.
  4. Enable the skill (toggle it on).
  5. Start a new Cowork session. Upload sample-deck-source.pptx.
  6. Paste the conversion prompt (from the starter kit) and run it.
  7. Compare the output to the baseline "out of the box" version.
  8. Extension: edit one slide's prompt to change the tone or layout. Regenerate just that slide.
Skill won't install? Check that the ZIP contains a folder with a SKILL.md at the root, not nested inside another folder. Kai is circulating during the exercise if you get stuck.

Exercise 2: Make it your own with Claude Design

Target time: 30 min · Uses: Claude Design (separate weekly bucket from chat)
  1. Open Claude Design on your Pro account.
  2. Set up a quick design system for a brand you invent or pick: your club, a fake coffee shop, an 80s arcade, a zine aesthetic, whatever. Two to three colors, a font vibe, a voice adjective.
  3. Use the same four-slide AI Kitchen content as the input.
  4. Let Claude Design regenerate the deck in your new identity.
  5. Swap screens with a neighbor. Compare.
  6. Reflection: same content, same model, very different output. What moved the needle?
The lesson: content stays constant, design system does the work. Design tokens are modular and swappable. Recipe 3 in the wild.
